My name is Sam Trejo—I am a quantitative social scientist interested in how social and biological factors combine to shape human development and the potential implications for public policy. I specialize in quasi-experimental, biosocial, and computational methods and am particularly interested in the reciprocal relationship between education and health across the life-course. My research capitalizes on two data sources that, until relatively recently, were largely unavailable to researchers: (1) large administrative datasets and (2) longitudinal studies containing molecular genetic data. Apart from puzzling over human behavior, I enjoy camping, bikes, board games, and Chinese food.
